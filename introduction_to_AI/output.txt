==================================
A - illustrating transition model
==================================

The "default" way I choose to implement agent players' actions is by a hungry strategy.
This is implemented by agents.HungryAgent() (see agents.py)
As it sounds, on every stage the agent iterates on all the possible (legal) available moves by calling GameState.action()
and choose the move that maximizing its score on the next stage. If there are multiple moves that causes the same
increment, the first move is selected according to their order it the moves list returned by GameState.action().

GameState uses bitmask operations to mask the legal movements in this clock direction order:
    - shift_up
    - shift_up_right
    - shift_right
    - shift_down_right
    - shift_down
    - shift_down_left
    - shift_left
    - shift_up_left
